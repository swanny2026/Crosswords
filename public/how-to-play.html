How to Play Crosswords

Crosswords is easy to learn but wonderfully satisfying to master. Unlike a traditional crossword with cryptic clues, here you reveal the hidden words by guessing letters. Here's everything you need to know to get started.

The basics

1

Look at the grid

You'll see an empty crossword grid made up of several interlocking words. All the letters are hidden — your job is to reveal them.

2

Guess a letter

Tap any letter on the keyboard to guess it. If that letter appears anywhere in the grid, every matching cell lights up instantly — for free.

3

Mind your guesses

If the letter isn't in the grid, you lose one guess. You start with a limited number, so think before you tap.

4

Complete the words

Each time you finish a word, you earn bonus guesses. Reveal every word in the grid to complete the puzzle.

The cascade effect

Here's where the strategy comes in. Because the words interlock and share letters, revealing one letter can uncover parts of several words at once. A single well-chosen guess can trigger a chain reaction across the whole grid. The most common letters — vowels like A, E, and O, and frequent consonants like R, S, T, and N — are often a smart place to start.

Building your streak

Every day there's a special Daily Challenge — a slightly harder puzzle that's the same for every player. Complete it each day to build your streak. The longer your streak, the higher you climb on the leaderboard. Miss a day and the streak resets, so it pays to come back daily.

Tip: Start with common vowels to open up the grid quickly, then use the letters you've revealed to work out which words are forming. The faster you solve it, the better your time on the leaderboard.

Competing with friends

Crosswords keeps track of your daily streak, the level you've reached, and your fastest completion times. Share your results with friends and family and challenge them to beat your time. It's a friendly way to keep a little daily rivalry going.

Tips to improve

  • Open with common vowels (A, E, O) to reveal lots of cells early.
  • Follow up with frequent consonants like R, S, T, N and L.
  • Once a few letters are showing, look for word patterns to deduce the rest.
  • Save risky guesses until you've narrowed down the possibilities.
  • Play every day to keep your streak alive and sharpen your speed.
